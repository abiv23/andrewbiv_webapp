Airline Customer Touch Points

Travel Industry Persona Mapping

A bad customer experience posted to social media can be detrimental to your brand.

Statistically: it takes 12 positive interactions to outweigh 1 negative interaction.

Timely communication can give voice to a quietly happy customer and help angry customers find the best solution to their problems.

Developing a customer net value score helps prioritize response and need in a poor customer service experience by treating each customer as a member of a cohort that has similiar interests and needs.

Data has been used historically in the airlines to guide decisions about fueling, crew schedules and flight itineraries. Today, customer journey analytics are used across the entire airline organization.

Analyzing each step of the customers journey informs what the best communication to your consumers will be at the exact time they need it. Texting a user their boarding pass as they arrive at the airport, or providing a timely update to a flight delay before they leave their house are customer touch point opportunities to differentiate and build customer loyalty. Southwest airlines has already seen the power of airline customer journey mapping.

Functions from marketing to operations, analytics, have a significant impact on costs and profitability. In response, Southwest launched “the Regular Operations Recovery Optimizer,” the first of its kind in the industry, the Optimizer’s ability to crunch data and quickly propose solutions may give Southwest “a unique competitive advantage.”
